Projects

Selected work

Research and development projects spanning wearable neuroimaging hardware, signal quality tooling, and clinical data analysis.

Brain-CAP High-Density Neuroimaging System
Aug 2021 – Present
NSF Funded · Python, MATLAB, STM32

Developed a high-density multimodal neuroimaging device in collaboration with the National Science Foundation and industry partners (Brain Products and Indus Instruments), capable of collecting data from 64 EEG electrodes, over 1,100 long-separation channels, and 256 short-separation fNIRS channels.

  • Performed statistical testing (paired t-test) using SciPy on resting-state data after preprocessing to validate brain signals against a gold-standard fNIRS device.
  • Conducted EEG frequency analysis to demonstrate strong alpha waves indicative of the resting state.
PHOEBE — fNIRS Data Quality Assessment Toolbox
Aug 2021
MATLAB, Lab Streaming Layer

Co-developed a MATLAB-based toolbox for real-time fNIRS data quality assessment, improving signal quality by extracting the cardiac band from raw signals and establishing quality metrics such as scalp coupling index and peak spectral power.

  • Automated the handling of real-time Lab Streaming Layer-streamed metadata from different manufacturers' devices by parsing information with the LSL library.
Dementia Risk Project
Dec 2023 – Present
NIH Funded · MATLAB, GitLab, E-Prime

Testing and maintaining fNIRS device (NIRx NIRScout) and digitizer tool (Polhemus Patriot) used in data collection, with routine inspection and troubleshooting to ensure equipment reliability throughout the research process.

  • Conducted data quality assessment post-collection using the QT-NIRS toolbox to increase accuracy and reliability of findings.
  • Performed subject- and group-level statistical analysis (mixed-effects model) to detect task-sensitive brain regions in dementia-risked women with Type 2 diabetes.
End of Life Project
Oct 2022
MD Anderson Cancer Center · MATLAB, EEGLAB, Clinical, R, Python

Preprocessed and analyzed clinical electrophysiological (EEG) data using MATLAB and Python to distinguish auditory responses in patients nearing end of life, applying parametric and non-parametric statistical tests to identify statistically significant responses against baseline noise at the single-subject level.

  • Set up and tested EEG equipment to record data from a near-death population.
  • Developed an analytical framework capable of handling diverse data challenges — poor quality, artifacts, and non-Gaussian distributions — enabling robust processing and interpretation.
Vital Signs Monitoring System Validation
Jan 2021
MATLAB, Arduino, MongoDB

Developed algorithms for a wireless vital-signs monitoring device capable of securely storing patient data — heart rate, temperature, and ECG — to a secured server.

  • Compared device performance to widely used medical devices for potential implementation in ICUs.
  • Conducted data analysis using statistical tests to validate device reliability.
